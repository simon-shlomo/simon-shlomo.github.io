Our services

Comprehensive mapping of drug-induced neurophysiological changes in brain activity.

NBT Analytics provides detailed neuroimaging insights for CNS drug development through state-of-the-art electroencephalography (EEG) analysis.

Our services for clinical trials are based on an EEG analysis pipeline developed at the VU University Amsterdam, which provides a comprehensive selection of neurophysiological biomarkers and uses machine-learning algorithms to identify highly sensitive indices of drug-induced changes in brain activity.

EEG biomarkers

Secondary endpoints for clinical trials

Electroencephalography (EEG) is a non-invasive, inexpensive and broadly available neuroimaging method that measures neurophysiological changes. The EEG is information rich and may give valuable insights on how a drug affects brain. EEG biomarkers, therefore, have great potential as secondary endpoints in clinical trials.

We provide analysis based on a comprehensive selection of EEG biomarkers in the temporal, spatial, and spectral domains. By mapping multiple EEG biomarkers we obtain a detailed overview of drug induced neurophysiological changes, that further can be integrated into highly sensitive indices (see below).

The EEG is particularly sensitive to alterations in the excitatory/inhibitory balance, which is compromised in several neuropsychiatric disorders 1. We have developed novel biomarkers that are sensitive to modulations of the excitatory/inhibitory balance and predict an important role of these biomarkers for understanding drug effects in humans2,3,4. One of these biomarkers is the oscillation burst duration biomarker, which has shown great sensitivity to a Alzheimer’s disease 2(see Figure)

Drug indices

Beyond single biomarkers

The EEG can be parameterized in multiple biomarkers, which complicates the assessment of drug-induced effects and leads to reduced statistical power.

We have developed an EEG analysis pipeline which identifies the right combination of multiple biomarkers and integrates these biomarkers into an index highly sensitive to a specific intervention or patient group5. The integrated index can for example be used for prognostic purposes or provide a single value quantification of drug effects for dosage studies. By combining multiple biomarkers into a single index we achieve a higher statistical power compared to a single biomarker based analysis (see Figure, simulated data).

About us

“We strive to improve and accelerate CNS drug development through innovative EEG analysis.”

NBT Analytics B.V. is a Dutch spin-off company from the VU University Amsterdam. The company was founded in August 2015 by Dr. Klaus Linkenkaer-Hansen, Dr. Simon-Shlomo Poil, and Medrithm Holding B.V. The company is funded by VU University Amsterdam and Medrithm Holding B.V.

NBT Analytics is build on the Neurophysiological Biomarker Toolbox (NBT), a research platform for M/EEG analysis developed at the VU University Amsterdam.
